Studio Projects

Snøhetta-Inspired Library — Architectural Site Design II

Boston University · AR528 · March 2026

Design of a public library as a symbolic "set of hands" within the urban fabric, inspired by a Snøhetta precedent study and a painting from my personal archive. The project operates across three scales: a city block, a building, and a room. Curved forms guide circulation, frame gathering spaces, and integrate the structure with its surrounding landscape.

Venice Exhibition Building — Architectural Site Design

BU Venice Academy · Fall 2025

Design of an exhibition space for an art school in Venice. The massing draws from the form of a Venetian gondola, translating its curved geometry into a sweeping roof structure. The lower level references the rhythm of canal-side facades; the upper level introduces continuous glass walls opening the exhibition spaces to light and the surrounding environment. Produced in AutoCAD with plan, section, and elevation, and realized as a physical model in wood, plexiglass, and cardboard.

Drawing Venice — Grand Canal Study

BU Venice Academy · Fall 2025

Large-scale observational drawing of the Grand Canal, approximately seven meters by two meters. Developed through close study of Venetian facade compositions, layering color and texture using pen, markers, and acrylic paint. Influenced by the material and spatial approach of Carlo Scarpa.

Ca' Pesaro & Ca' Dario — Analytical Diagrams

BU Venice Academy · Fall 2025

Structural element diagrams of Ca' Pesaro and fenestration diagrams of Ca' Dario, two landmark palazzos on the Grand Canal. Work focused on understanding Venetian Gothic and Renaissance construction logic.
